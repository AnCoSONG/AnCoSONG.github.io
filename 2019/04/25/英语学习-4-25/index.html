英语学习 4/25

发表于 2019-04-25 | 更新于: 2019-04-27 | 分类于 日常生活 | 阅读次数:
字数统计: 1,871 | 阅读时长 ≈ 9

You cannot win the war against the world, if you cannot win the war against your own mind.

单词

  • perception

    知觉；认知；感觉；看法;洞察力

    • the way you think about or understand someone or something

      often + of

    • the ability to understand or notice something easily

      洞察力

  • alter

    • to change something + object object

    • to make change something to make it fit well

  • radical

    more radical most radical

    • very new and different from what is traditional or ordinary

      根本的；彻底的;激进的; from 有道

    • very basic and important

      根本上的

    • radically: adverb

  • intently

    专业地;一心一意地;心无旁骛地

  • impatiently

    不耐烦的；焦躁的 from 有道

    以下内容from 韦氏

    • not willing to wait for something or someone: not patient often + for 但是也可以跟with/at/to搭配

    • wanting or eager to do something without waiting: 有想做的意思

    • showing your lack of patience

  • await

    等待；等候

    • wait for: no object

  • obscura: 可能使打错了或者是相机的名字,找不到

  • pinhole

    针孔

  • topographical

    地质学的；地形学；

  • ancestor

    祖先

  • patent

    from 有道

    v.授予专利；取得专利

    adj.专利的；显然的

    n.专利权

    from 韦氏

    • adjective:

      • of or relating to patents

      • obvious or clear

    • noun:

      • an official document that gives a person or a company the right to be the only one that makes or sells a product for a certain period of time.

        • count

          • often + on

          • often + for

        • noncount

  • duplicate

    from 有道

    • v. 复制;使加倍
    • n. 副本
    • adj. 复制的

    from 韦氏

    • verb
      • to make an exact copy of (something)
      • to produce (something) in another form

  • etching

    from 有道

    • n. 蚀刻术；
    • v. 蚀刻(etch的ing形式)
    • adjective. 蚀刻的

    from 韦氏

    • to produce a pattern, design or something by using a powerful liquid(called an acid) to cut the surface of metal or glass.

      often used figuratively, means 铭记

  • engraving

    from 有道

    • 雕刻；雕刻术
    • 在…雕刻；给…留下深刻印象(engrave的ing形式)

    from 韦氏

    • +obejct to cut or carve lines, letters, designs, etc., onto or into a hard surface

      often used figuratively means 铭记

  • equivalent

    from 有道

    • adjective. 等价的;相等的; n.等价物;相等物;

    from 韦氏

    • having the same value, use, meaning, etc.

      often + to

  • prolific

    from 有道

    • 多产的//参看韦氏解释 并非类似abundant的意思

    from 韦氏

    • produce a large amount of something

  • destine

    from 有道

    • 注定；命定；

    from 韦氏

    • certain to do or to be something

    • certain to achieve or experience something : certain to have a particular job, status, etc. + for

  • multitude

    from 有道

    • n. 群众；多数

    from 韦氏

    • noun: a great number of things or people //对应”很多人”的意思

    • often + of : 对应”很多”的意思

    • the multitude: 群众

  • successor

    这个涉及到succeed的一个意思，succeed不光有成功的意思，还有接替

    from 有道

    • 继承者；后续的事物

    from 韦氏

    • a person who has a job, position, or title after someone else: someone who succeeds another person

  • rapid

    from 有道

    • adj. 迅速的；急促的；飞快的；
    • n.急流；高速交通工具；

  • emulsion

    from 有道

    • n. 乳剂；乳状液；感光乳剂

  • eliminate

    from 有道

    • vt. 消除;排除

  • pictorial

    from 有道

    • adj. 绘画的；形象化的；n.画报；画刊

  • composition

    from 有道

    • 作文；作曲；作品；构成；合成物；成分

  • democratic

    from 有道

    • 民主的；民主政治的；大众的

  • authenticity

    from 有道

    • 真实性；确实性；可靠性

  • genuineness

    from 有道

    • 真正；真实；真诚；真挚

  • accidental

    from 有道

    • adjective: 意外的；偶然的
    • noun: 次要方面；非主要特性；

  • surprising

    令人惊讶的；意外的；

    from 韦氏: unexpected or unusual

  • amazing

    令人惊异的；不可思议的；惊奇的；

    from 韦氏: causing great surprise or wonder

  • unintentional

    无意识的；非故意的

    from 韦氏

    • not done in a way that is planned or intended: not intentional

  • obsolete

    • 过时的；废弃的

    • from 韦氏

      • no longer used because something newer exists

      • replaced by something newer

      • no longer used by anyone

  • medium

    • (传播手段)媒介；
    • 中等的

TPO 精读

TPO22 The birth of photography

长难句翻译

  • Perceptions of the visible world were greatly altered by the invention of photography in the middle of the nineteenth century.

    对于可见世界的认识，被十九世纪中叶摄影的发明彻底改变

  • The images produced by the camera obscura, a boxlike device that used a pinhole or lens to throw an image onto a ground-glass screen or a piece of white paper, were already familiar—the device had been much employed by topographical artists like the Italian painter Canaletto in his detailed views of the city of Venice.

    相机Obscura，一个盒状的设备，使用针孔或者镜头将画面投射到磨砂玻璃屏幕或者一张白纸上。它拍摄的照片已经很亲切熟悉。这个设备已经被地形艺术家Canaletto在它对于威尼斯市的详细描述中广泛的使用了。

    非常长，非常多的定语后置是托福阅读的常态，提主干很关键.
