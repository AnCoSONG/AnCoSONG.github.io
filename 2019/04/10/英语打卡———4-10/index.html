You can’t win the war against the world if you can’t win the war against your own mind.

Self-discipline is the center of every material success.

单词

  1. twist：扭；扭曲的；
  2. ankle：n. 脚踝
  3. sketch：a quick, rough drawing that shows the main features of an object.
  4. controversial：非常有争议的;
  5. controversy：争论焦点;
  6. artifact：艺术品
  7. problematic：有问题的;
  8. tapestry：挂毯;
  9. commission：定做；to order or request (something) to be made or done；
  10. bishop：基督教主教
  11. heir：继承人;
  12. throne：君王宝座；王位；
  13. Embroiderers：织布工人
  14. depict：描绘
  15. thesis：论文；论点
  16. senior thesis：学士论文；A senior thesis is a large, independent research project that students take on during their senior year of high school or college to fulfill their graduation requirement.
  17. certification：证书
  18. enroll in：参加；选课
  19. seminar：研讨会
  20. sabbatical：休假
  21. appeal：吸引力；

TPO 听力精听

TPO 28 Conversation 1

Listen to a conversation between a student and a professor.

S: I am so sorry I am late professor Miles.I just finished that(X) at(√) student medical center. I twisted my uncle(X) ankle(对) play soccer this morning. I took longer than I expected to see the doctor.

P: That’s Okay.Don’t worried about it,David.So let’s get started. You paper on xxx’s political philosophy has few issues I’d like to cover. You give a great biographical sketch at the beginning, Ok. But then as you get into political philosophy, I don’t think you’ve done enough to situate his philosophy with the time period. In other word, you haven’t connected xxx’s philosophy to the thinking of other intellectuals of the time.

S：So I haven’t capture the critical influence, the influences that were most significant to his political thinking.

TPO 35 Conversation 2 精听

写在纸上

TPO 28 Conversation 2 精听

Listen to a conversation between the student and his academic advisor.

Hi, sorry I am late, Professor Blane.

No problem,Jim.

So you got some problem about your senior thesis requirement?

Yeah, I have got a couple of problem actually.

So, the first thing is, you normally write it during the first half of the academic year, Right? In your final year of study?

Right

But I have my students teaching for that time. I want to teach high school English after graduation. So I really need to give that to full my attention. And I just worry that I won’t be able to if I am writing my senior thesis at same time. I mean it’s supposed to be 35 to 50 pages. That’s a serious commitment.

You’re right. But it really isn’t a problem.

Really?

No. A lot English majors get teacher certification. So we have students like you do their senior thesis after their students teaching. It works out well because many students want to use a unit they taught as the basis of their paper. So you’ll just enroll in a thesis seminar for the second semester.

Well That is a big relief. But it brings to my second problem.

I’d really focus my study on old and Middle English literature.(我真的想将我研究的重点放在古老的中世纪的英语文学) I am even thinking about doing a graduate degree with the concentration in that after I taught for a while.(我甚至想要在我教了一段时间书后，在哪方面拿一个研究生学位) So I was hoping to do my senior thesis on Chaucer, on the Canterbury Tales, because that would obviously be useful if I do go on but. But…

Uh…But professor Johnson

Exactly. Professor Johnson is going to taking a sabbatical to do research in France during the second half of the year.（是的。John教授在下半年会到法国，利用休假去作研究。） So without him around, I am not sure how I could do a senior thesis on the Canterbury Tales. I mean the focus of his teaching and research is unique around here. Yes, I understand. It would be difficult for you to do the paper without Professor Johnson around. Hmmm, would you allowed me to try to sell you an alternate plan? （你允许我给你推荐一个备选方案嘛）

Well, You can try. But Chauser is sort of My hero, you know what I mean.

Well, I am teaching a course on literature of the Renaissance in the first half of the year. It’ll meet late in the day, so it will not interface with you teaching. And I haven’t offer in a quit while now so I doubt you ever studied that period on the college level.

No I haven’t.

If you be interested in taking the course, I would be happy to give you supplemental reading. And I’d also be happy to be your advisor for you paper later on.

Well, I never looked at that area before, but I always had an interest in it. So that does have a certain appeal.

Well, if you do decide to go this route, I would make that decision soon and I would use this summer productively. After all, this is not going to be like taking an intro course.
